# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

Primary: recruiters and hiring managers evaluating Jishnu's data/analytics engineering profile — often mid-task, skimming, deciding in under a minute whether to read the CV properly. Secondary: engineering peers who land on a project or post, and AI agents (recruiter tooling, answer engines) that read the site programmatically — the site treats agents as a first-class audience (llms.txt, markdown variants, server-rendered everything).

## Product Purpose

jishnuteegala.com is Jishnu Teegala's personal site: home, CV (with downloadable build-generated PDFs), live projects showcase, labs, scaffolded blog, and design system. Success: a recruiter leaves with a clear, credible picture of his work and downloads or shares the CV; an agent can extract the same picture without executing JavaScript.

## Positioning

A precise, self-evidently well-made personal site — the site itself is proof of the engineering judgment the CV claims.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary CTA: read/download the CV.
- Secondary CTA: email hi@jishnuteegala.com.
- The line a visitor remembers: this person builds carefully.
- Belief ladder: (1) this site is unusually well made → (2) the work listed is real and current (live GitHub data) → (3) the CV is worth my time → (4) worth contacting.
- Proof on hand: pinned projects with live repo metadata; the CV content itself; the site's own craft.

## Brand Personality

Quiet, precise, credible. Typography-led restraint — the confidence of not decorating. One signature flourish maximum. No exclamation marks, no emoji waves, no self-hype; copy states facts plainly.

## Anti-references

- Terminal/phosphor "hacker" aesthetics.
- Generic AI-portfolio grammar: hero metrics, skills badge clouds, identical card grids, eyebrow labels on every section, gradient text.
- Corporate SaaS landing-page tropes; anything that reads as a template.

## Design Principles

1. The site is the portfolio — every detail (perf, semantics, a11y) is itself evidence.
2. One source of truth, many projections — CV data drives page, PDFs, markdown; nothing drifts.
3. Agents are readers too — server-rendered, semantic, stable URLs; no content behind JS.
4. Typography does the design — hierarchy from type and space, not boxes and colour.
5. Earn every element — if it doesn't inform, it goes.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA as the working bar: 4.5:1 body contrast, full keyboard navigation, prefers-reduced-motion honoured (flourish included), semantic HTML throughout. Light, dark, and system themes.
